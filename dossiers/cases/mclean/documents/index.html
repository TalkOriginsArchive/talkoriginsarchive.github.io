McLean v. Arkansas Documentation Project

Route: /dossiers/cases/mclean/documents/ · TalkOrigins feature page.

McLean v. Arkansas Documentation Project material is being staged from the local antievolution.org content archive into the TalkOrigins modern dossier structure.

The Drupal-rendered McLean pages are the primary coverage source because they expose the fullest set of testimony, deposition, line-numbered transcript, decision, and legal-document pages. The older static project tree remains the provenance and organization cross-check.

This index is a staging surface. Document links below now resolve to modernized document pages extracted from the Drupal-rendered McLean corpus. Each page remains queued for body extraction, provenance, copyright/permission, and responsive review before translation or broader reuse.

Known Coverage Notes

  • The original project index identified plaintiff transcript pages before page 62 as missing. Drupal nodes 714 and 715 supply preliminary-OCR pages that fill part of that gap and selected later pages, but they still need proofreading.
  • Francisco Ayala's trial testimony is identified in the project source as missing from the plaintiff transcript set.
  • Defense-side trial testimony is incomplete; some material is represented by notes, excerpts, and depositions rather than official transcript pages.
  • The legal-materials project page lists additional filings that are not present in the local public tree. The complaint and joint stipulation are now rendered from the static project source; the other listed filings remain acquisition leads.
  • Permission-limited excerpts require workbench review before publication or translation.
  • Geisler materials are link-only for public cross-domain use. This poc3 index links to the relevant antievolution.org pages and does not re-host those pages under talkorigins.org.

Completeness Leads

  • Archive.org copy of supplemental pages 1-36 preserves the omitted Drupal book-export version.
  • Archive.org copy of supplemental pages, part 2 preserves the omitted Drupal book-export version.
  • NCSE overview of McLean v. Arkansas supplies a stable secondary overview and case context.
  • Justia copy of the district-court decision is a public legal reference for citation comparison.
  • UA Little Rock Center for Arkansas History and Culture manuscript collections lists Judge William R. Overton papers with McLean trial briefs, exhibits, depositions, notes, and correspondence.
  • University of Arkansas Special Collections manuscript collections remain a research lead for the older cited Creation Science Papers, Manuscript Collection 701.
