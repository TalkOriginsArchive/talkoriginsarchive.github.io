.? Ussher, James
[1581-1656] Arzobispo de Armagh, primado de toda Irlanda. Creacionista. Autor de "The annals of the world deduced from the origin of time, and continued to the beginning of the Emperour Vespasians reign, and the totall destruction and abolition of the temple and common-wealth of the Jews: containing the historie of the Old and New Testament, with that of the Macchabees, also the most memorable affairs of Asia and Egypt, and the rise of the empire of the Roman Caesars under C. Julius, and Octavianus: collected from all history, as well sacred, as prophane, and methodically digested," en el que dedujo la fecha de la Creación en el año 4004 a. C., a partir del análisis de genealogías bíblicas. Un comentario interesante sobre esto aparece en el ensayo de Gould "Fall of the House of Ussher", in his collection "Eight Little Piggies" (Penguin 1993).
