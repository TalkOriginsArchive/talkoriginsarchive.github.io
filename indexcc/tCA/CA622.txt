BriefClaim: Without a literal Fall, there is no need for Jesus and redemption.
Title: Without a literal Fall
Keywords: redemption, sin, suffering, Fall

Claim:

Without a literal Fall, there is no need for redemption and thus no need
for Jesus or Christianity.

Source:

Grant, Heber J., Anthony W. Ivins, and Charles W. Nibley. n.d.
 Mormon view of evolution. 
 https://web.archive.org/web/20020816160616if_/http://www.lightplanet.com/mormons/basic/gospel/creation/evolution_eom.htm
Morris, Henry M. 1998.  The fall, the curse, and evolution.  _Back
 to Genesis_ 112 (Apr.).
 http://www.icr.org/index.php?module=articles&action=view&ID=837

Response:

1. It is sin in general, and not merely one particular instance of sin,
   that makes redemption necessary.  If you can find any sin in the
   world, then the claim is baseless.  Proof of this is given by the fact
   that many Christians feel the need for redemption but do not believe in
   a literal Fall.

2. This claim implies that sin and redemption are about things that
   happened thousands of years ago, not about anything happening to us
   today.  It makes religion less relevant to people's lives.

3. Origins are not determined by our personal decisions of what religion
   to follow.
