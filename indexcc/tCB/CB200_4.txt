BriefClaim: The immune system is irreducibly complex.
Title: Immune system and Irreducibly Complexity
Keywords: irreducible complexity, immune system, design.

Claim:

The human immune system is irreducibly complex, indicating that
it must have been designed.

Source:

DBB, pp. 117-139.


Response:

1. The complement system of the human immune system is not irreducibly
   complex.  Urochordates have a functional complement system, yet they
   lack a component of the cascade.

2. Common mechanisms, such as gene duplication and co-option of molecules
   with other roles, allow the immune system to evolve naturally.  Much
   has been written on the subject. (Kasahara et al. 1997; Lindsay 1999;
   Travis 1998)
#   Klein and Nikolaidis (2005) give detailed evidence of its evolution,
#   concluding that "the AIS [antibody-based immune system] is an example
#   of an organ system that has evolved gradually through a series of small
#   steps over an extended period."

3. Behe gets some of the basic biology wrong.  Bacteria are not destroyed,
   as Behe says (1996, 134), by water rushing in when the cell membrane is
   punctured, but because their chemical gradients have been destroyed
   (Ussery 1999).

4. Irreducible complexity <CB200>is not an obstacle to evolution> and
   <CI102>doesn't imply design>.
