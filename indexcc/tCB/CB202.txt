BriefClaim: No gradual biochemical evolution models have been published.
Title: Articles on gradual biochemical evolution 
Keywords: gradual evolution, publish, article, biochemistry, model

Claim:

Professional literature is silent on the subject of the evolution of
biochemical systems.

Source:

DBB, 68, 72, 97, 114, 115-116, 138, 185-186.

Response:

1. The claim is simply false.  Dozens of articles exist on the subjects
   for which Behe claims the literature is missing.  David Ussery, for
   example, found 107 articles on cilia evolution, 125 on flagella
   evolution, 27 on the evolution of the entire coagulation system, 130 on
   the evolution of vesicle transport, 84 on "molecular evolution of the
   immune system." (Ussery 1998)

2. Behe tries to make his claim appear more dramatic by overstating our
   understanding of the molecular workings of the cell.  For example, he
   says (p. 232), "Over the past four decades modern biochemistry has
   uncovered the secrets of the cell."  But our understanding has only
   just begun.  In the years since Behe wrote his book, journals have been
   filled with thousands of research articles uncovering new information,
   and much remains to be uncovered.  When the complete _Escherichia coli_
   genome was sequenced in 1998, the functions of a third of its genes
   were still completely unknown, and _E. coli_ is much simpler than human
   cells.

3. Behe's work on intelligent design theory has produced no publications
   in scientific literature.  In fact, there have been no scientific
   publications on intelligent design by any of its proponents (Gilchrist
   1997).
