BriefClaim: Evolution can't explain butterfly evolving from caterpillar.
Title: Butterfly from caterpillar
Keywords: metamorphosis, butterfly, monarch, incredulity.

Claim:

Evolution can't explain a butterfly evolving from a caterpillar.

Source:

Brown, Walt, ~2000.  Twenty questions for evolutionists.
 https://web.archive.org/web/20010413023343if_/http://www.linda.net/question.html

Response:

1. Butterflies don't evolve from caterpillars; butterflies develop from
   caterpillars.  How it happens is a problem in developmental biology,
   not evolutionary biology.  It is akin to the problem of how adult
   humans develop from embryos.  It happens every day, so it obviously is
   not a theoretical difficulty.

   Fruit flies go through the same developmental stages as caterpillars
   and butterflies, and the research on fruit fly genetics is very
   extensive.  Anyone who is interested in how butterflies develop is
   advised to look in that research.

2. The <CB311>evolution of metamorphosis> is not a problem, either.
