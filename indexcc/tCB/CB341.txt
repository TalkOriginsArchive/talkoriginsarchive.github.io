BriefClaim: Snake venom and hollow fangs could not have evolved simultaneously.
Title: Evolution of snake fangs
Keywords: coevolution.

Claim:

Venomous snakes have hollow fangs and venom glands.  Evolution would
have had to produce both of these innovations together, since either would
be pointless without the other.

Source:

Response:

1. Venom is not pointless without hollow fangs.  Gila monsters have venom
   glands without hollow fangs.  Other venomous snakes have furrowed
   fangs.  These show plausible intermediate forms to hollow-fanged
   venomous snakes:
    a. A nonvenomous snake already has teeth which break the skin of its
       victim.
    b. Poisonous saliva becomes advantageous to some snakes because it
       can get into the victim and kill it faster.
    c. Furrowed fangs allow the venom to be concentrated in the prey.
    d. The furrows gradually become enclosed, resulting in hollow fangs.
