BriefClaim: Endorphins at death indicate a beneficent creator, not evolution.
Title: Endorphins at death
Keywords: endorphins, trauma, mercy

Claim:

The body releases endorphins at death, making dying less traumatic.
Evolution cannot explain this, because there is no selection pressure for
a biological mechanism to help people die peacefully.  But it does show
evidence of a merciful creator.

Source:

Easterbrook, Gregg, 9 Dec. 2003.  NFL teams should hire psychics, and why,
 why, why are you punting?!
 https://web.archive.org/web/20070210082554if_/http://www.nfl.com/nflnetwork/story/6908185

Response:

1. Endorphin levels are elevated even by relatively minor trauma.
   Alleviating the pain and stress of a person who has suffered a trauma
   can keep them functional enough to take life-saving actions, such as
   staunching the bleeding or seeking help.  That
   endorphins are released also during fatal trauma is a side effect.

2. Release of endorphins during trauma is not entirely merciful.  If the
   person survives the trauma, the withdrawal from the endorphins can
   produce emotional distress that can contribute to post-traumatic stress
   disorder or alcohol addiction (Volpicelli et al. 1999).

#3. Endorphins may not decrease a person's perception of pain.  In one
#   study of trauma victims, there was no correlation between pain
#   perception and beta endorphin levels (Bernstein et al. 1995).
#1. Bernstein L, Garzone PD, Rudy T, Kramer B, Stiff D, Peitzman A., 1995.
#   Pain perception and serum beta-endorphin in trauma patients.
#   _Psychosomatics_ 36(3): 276-284.
