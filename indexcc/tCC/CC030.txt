BriefClaim: All human fossils would fit on a billiard table.
Title: Human fossils on a table
Keywords: hominid, physical anthropology.

Claim:

All known fossils of ancient humans would fit on a billiard table (or in a
coffin).

Source:

SciCre, 202.

LifeHow, 86.

Response:

1. That may have been true at one time, but there are thousands of hominid
   fossils now.  Lubenow (1992) found that there were fossils from almost
   4,000 hominid individuals catalogued as of 1976.  As of 1999, there
   were fossils of about 150 _Homo erectus_ individuals, 90
   _Australopithecus robustus_, 150 _Australopithecus afarensis_, 500
   Neanderthals, and more (Handprint 1999).  Foley (2004) lists some of
   the more prominent fossils.

2. It takes only a handful of fossils to show that hominid forms have
   changed over time.
