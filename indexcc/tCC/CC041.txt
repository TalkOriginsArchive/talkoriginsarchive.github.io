BriefClaim: Homo habilis is an invalid taxon.
Title: Validity of _Homo habilis_
Keywords: Homo habilis, Australopithecus, taxon

Claim:

_Homo habilis_ is an invalid taxon.  It is a taxonomic waste bin
in which several different types have been placed.

Source:

Lubenow, Marvin, 1992. _Bones of Contention: A creationist assessment of
 the human fossils_. Grand Rapids, MI: Baker Books, pp. 157-166.

Response:

1. At least two different species have been assigned to _Homo habilis_,
   but that does not make the taxon invalid.  OH7, its type specimen
   (which defines the taxon), clearly differs from other named species.
   As more fossils are found, we will get a better idea of the ranges of
   variation and thus of what constitutes different species.
