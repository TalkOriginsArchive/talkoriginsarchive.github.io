BriefClaim: KP 271 (a fossil humerus) was human.
Title: KP 271 (a fossil humerus) was human.
Keywords: Australopithecus

Claim:

The fossil humerus KP 271 is an apparently human fossil from four million
years ago, which, according to the standard evolutionary model, is well
before the appearance of modern humans.

Source:

Lubenow, Marvin L. 1992. _Bones of Contention: A creationist assessment
 of the human fossils_. Grand Rapids, MI: Baker Books, pp. 54-58.

Response:

1. Even a humerus from a chimpanzee looks similar to a human humerus; it
   should not be surprising that the humerus from a closer relative would
   look even more similar.  However, the anatomical evidence strongly
   indicates that the specimen is not human and is a good match with
   _Australopithecus anamensis_ (Lague and Jungers 1996).

Links:
