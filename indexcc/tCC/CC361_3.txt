BriefClaim: Contorted positions of fossil animals indicate rapid burial.
Title: Contorted Fossils
Keywords: fossil, catastrophism.

Claim:

The contorted positions of fossil animals indicate that they were rapidly
buried alive.

Source:

Brown, Walt, 1995. _In the Beginning: Compelling evidence for creation
 and the flood_, 6th ed.  Phoenix, AZ: Center for Scientific Creation, p. 9.
 https://web.archive.org/web/20240413100615if_/http://www.creationscience.com/onlinebook/LifeSciences25.html

Response:

1. As carcasses dry, ligaments contract and distort the body (Weber
   1980).  Also, dead animals are often disturbed by scavengers
   and/or water currents before their remains become buried.  This can
   account for the contorted positions.

2. Some fossils do form by rapid burial, but these indicate only local
   catastrophes, such as landslides of a river bank.
