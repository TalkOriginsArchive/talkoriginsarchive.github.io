BriefClaim: There should be transitionals among living creatures.
Title: Transitionals among living creatures
Keywords: transitional fossil

Claim:

There should be transitionals among living creatures, not just between
fossils.
[Subsumed by CB805]

Source:

Brown20.

Response:

1. This claim stems from not understanding how the process of evolution is
   supposed to work.  According to the theory, any two species share a
   common ancestor, but the more remote in time that ancestor was, the
   more those species have diverged.  The transitions occurred over time,
   and most, perhaps all, of the intermediates to a remote ancestor would
   have gone extinct.

2. In some groups, such as many grasses and leafhoppers, there are "living
   transitionals," or lots of different species which are hard to tell
   apart.  This is due to a relatively recent divergence where the
   intermediate forms have not gone extinct.
