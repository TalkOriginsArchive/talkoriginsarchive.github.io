Title: C14 in diamonds
Keywords: carbon-14, radiometric dating

Claim:

Diamonds contain significant amounts of carbon-14.  Essentially all of the
C14 would have decayed if the diamonds were older than 100,000 years old,
but the diamonds are considered much older than that.

Source:

https://web.archive.org/web/20081015140351if_/http://www.icr.org/research/AGUC-14_Poster_Baumgardner.pdf

Response:

1. Three other sources for the C14 measurements must be considered.
    - The inherent imprecision of the measurement.
    - Contamination from outside sources, such as as from people handling
      the diamonds.
    - Sources of C14 other than from atmospheric N14.  Diamonds contain
      trace amounts of nitrogen which may be converted to C14 by
      background radiation within the earth (see <CD011.6>CD011.6>
      regarding C14 in coal).
