Title: Movement of tectonic plates
Keywords: plate tectonics, GPS

Claim:

There are inconsistencies in the measurements of continental plate
movement, calling the theory of plate tectonics into question.  In
particular, South America is not moving west as fast as North America, as
we would expect if the Mid-Atlantic ridge were spreading evenly.  Some
maps show it moving north or even east.

Source:

CreationWiki. 2005 (27 June).  Continental drift.
 https://web.archive.org/web/20060301153445if_/http://www.nwcreation.net/wiki/index.php?title=Continental_drift

Berg, Randy. 2004.  Is the Mid-Atlantic Ridge still spreading?
 https://web.archive.org/web/20250416235208if_/http://www.earthage.org/continentaldrft/Drift%20Data.htm

Response:

1. The inconsistencies are in expectations of what to expect from theory
   of plate tectonics.  The plates are not inflexible.  Often they deform,
   producing, for example, terrain such as the Basin and Range area of the
   western United States.  The western edge of South America is being
   deformed to the east by collision with the Nazca Plate.

   Furthermore, there is nothing that says the spread of the Mid-Atlantic
   Ridge should be even along the whole of its length.  The plates move
   across the surface of a sphere, not on a plane as the maps depict.
   This means the spreading cannot be uniform along the length of a rift
   (without deforming the plates).  Plates can (and do) spin a bit as well
   as slide to the side.
