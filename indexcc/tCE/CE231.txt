BriefClaim: Jupiter and Saturn are cooling too rapidly to be old.
Title: Jupiter and Saturn cooling
Keywords: radiation, Jupiter, primordial heat

Claim:

Jupiter and Saturn are cooling, giving off their internal heat at a rate
too great for them to be billions of years old.

Source:

Brown, Walt. 1995.  _In the Beginning: Compelling evidence for creation
 and the Flood_.  Phoenix, AZ: Center for Scientific Creation, p. 30.

Response:

1. Jupiter is cooling slowly enough that it could still be radiating its
   primordial heat.  Saturn's extra heat could come from gravitational
   potential energy as helium in its atmosphere condenses into droplets
   and falls toward the center.
