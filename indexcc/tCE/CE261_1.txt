BriefClaim: Oort cloud and Kuiper belt are ad hoc fantasies of astronomers.
Title: Reality of Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud
Keywords: young earth

Claim:

The Kuiper Belt and Oort Cloud have never been observed.  They have been
proposed merely as an _ad hoc_ explanation for a source of comets.

Source:

Brown, Walt, 1995.  _In the Beginning: Compelling evidence for creation
 and the Flood_.  Phoenix, AZ: Center for Scientific Creation, p. 69.

Response:

1. As of June 2000, more than 250 objects in the Kuiper Belt have been
   observed directly (Buie 2000), and it alone can be the source of
   short-term comets.

   The Oort cloud has not been observed directly (although Sedna, a
   planetoid discovered in March 2004, might be in the Oort cloud), but
   its presence is well supported based on observations of long-period
   comets.

2. If there were no source for new comets to come from, all comets would
   have the same age.  They do not.  Some are young and have lots of
   gasses; others are little more than gravel heaps.
