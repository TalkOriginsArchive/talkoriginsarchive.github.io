BriefClaim: The sun has most of the mass but little angular momentum of the solar system.
Title: Sun's small angular momentum
Keywords: angular momentum, magnetohydrodynamics

Claim:

The sun has 99 percent of the mass of the solar system, but less than 1
percent of the angular momentum.  It is spinning too slowly to have formed
naturally.

Source:

Brown, Walt, 1995.  _In the Beginning: Compelling evidence for creation
 and the Flood_.  Phoenix, AZ: Center for Scientific Creation, p. 19.

Response:

1. Among solar-type stars, there is a strong correlation between age and
   rotation rate; the younger stars spin more rapidly (Baliunas et
   al. 1995).  This implies some kind of braking mechanism that slows a
   star's rotation.  A likely candidate is an interaction between the
   star's magnetic field and its solar wind (Parker 1965).
