BriefClaim: Physicists found that the speed of light was once faster.
Title: Physicists Say Light was Faster
Keywords: speed of light, uniformity.

Claim:

Physicists Joao Magueijo at London's Imperial College and Andreas Albrecht
at U. C. Davis say the speed of light immediately after the beginning of
the universe was many times faster than it is now, and it has been slowing
ever since.

Source:

Dolphin, Lambert, 1999.  On the constancy of the speed of light.
 http://www.ldolphin.org/constc.shtml

Response:

1. Albrecht and Magueijo [1999] have hypothesized that the speed of light
   may have been much faster (up to 10^69 times faster) in the very early
   universe.  This is a theoretical model proposed as an alternative to
   the inflation theory of the early universe to explain why the universe
   is so uniform.  The increased speed applies only to the inflationary
   stage of Big Bang models, which means only the first 10^-43 second or
   so.  Their theory has not been verified by observation.

2. Other evidence shows that the <CE411>speed of light has been
   constant> for a long time.
