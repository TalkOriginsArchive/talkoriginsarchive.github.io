BriefClaim: Short-lived isotopes Th-230 and U-236 exist on the moon.
Title: U-236 and Th-230 on the moon.
Keywords: radionucleotide

Claim:

Th-230 and U-236 exist on the moon.  But these isotopes are so short-lived
that we would not expect any still to be around if the moon were billions
of years old.

Source:

Wysong, R. L., 1976.  _The Creation-Evolution Controversy_, Midland, MI:
 Inquiry Press, pp. 177-178.

Response:

1. Both nucleotides are formed by ongoing processes.
   <sup>230</sup>Th is an intermediate decay product of
   <sup>238</sup>U, which has a half-life of 4.468 billion years.
   <sup>236</sup>U is produced in trace amounts by the capture of slow
   neutrons.
