BriefClaim: The first known languages are highly complex.
Title: Complex early language
Keywords: language

Claim:

The first known human languages were already very complex.  Languages do
not show the evolutionary progression we would expect if humans evolved
gradually.

Source:

Skjaerlund, David, n.d.  Creationism explains human diversity.
 http://www.forerunner.com/forerunner/X0722_Creationism_explains.html


Response:

1. The first known languages were written languages (else they would not
   be known).  Since most cultures in the world have had no written
   language, and most people have been illiterate even where written
   language existed, written language is a poor metric to use to measure
   language in general.  Language had been developing for an unknown
   period of time before written language evolved.

2. The earliest known writing is simpler than written languages today.
   There are very simple, nonlinguistic precursors (no grammar) to
   cuneiform writing (Coulmas 1989).
