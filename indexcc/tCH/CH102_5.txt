BriefClaim: God's promise of no more floods fails if Noah's flood was local.
Title: God's promise and local floods.
Keywords: flood, deluge, local, global, covenant

Claim:

Noah's flood must have been global, not a local flood like some propose.
God promised that he would send no more floods (Gen. 9:8-17), but there
have been many local floods through history.  To believe in a local flood
is to say that God broke his promise.

Source:

Ham, Ken, Jonathan Sarfati, and Carl Wieland, 2000.  Was the Flood global?
 from: _The Revised and Expanded Answers Book_.  Green Forest AR: Master
 Books, ch. 10.
 http://www.answersingenesis.org/Home/Area/AnswersBook/global10.asp

Response:

1. According to the Bible, God promises not to send another flood that
   would destroy all life.  Since no such flood has occurred, the promise
   is kept.

2. Someone who proposes that Genesis refers to a local flood has already
   decided not to interpret the Bible literally, so a non-literal
   interpretation of the promise is not an issue.  Non-literal
   interpretations of the Bible are compatible both with the Bible and
   with reality; literal interpretations are <CH102>scarcely compatible
   with the Bible> and not compatible with reality.
