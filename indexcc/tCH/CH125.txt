Title: Jabal al Lawz as Mt. Sinai
Keywords: Arabia, Sinai, Moses, roof pendant

Claim:

The mountain Jabal al Lawz in Arabia is Mt. Sinai.  The heat-darkened
rocks at its summit show evidence of having been melted or charred by
Biblical events.

Source:

Cornuke, B. and D. Halbrook, 2000.  _In Search of The Mountain of
 God_.  Broadman & Holman Publishers, Nashville, Tennessee.

Response:

1. The dark-colored rocks at the summit of Jabal al Lawz are roof pendants
   of native dark greenstone intruded by lighter granite (Bramkamp et al.
   1963; Trent and Johnson 1967).  If they look heated, it is because they
   consist of metamorphosed lava, albeit lava that cooled over a billion
   years ago.

2. The Bible tells how long the Israelites traveled to Mt. Sinai.  At a
   reasonable rate of travel, Mt. Sinai would have to be in the northern
   Sinai Peninsula, not in Saudi Arabia (Wood 2000).  Furthermore, Exodus
   18:27 implies that a Midianite was not in his own country (i.e., not in
   Arabia) while they were camped near Mt. Sinai.
