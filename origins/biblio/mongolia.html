TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Mongolia

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/mongolia.html

Mongolia serves as a critical region for understanding Late Cretaceous ecosystems and dinosaur evolution, particularly through the rich fossil deposits of the Nemegt and Djadokhta formations. This bibliography compiles paleontological literature on theropods, ornithischians, and other vertebrates found in this area, highlighting its importance for deep time research and biogeography. The citations focus on scientific descriptions and anatomical studies rather than creationist arguments.

Abstract

Late Cretaceous dinosaurs have been discovered along the Pacific margin of Baja California. The presence of Hypacrosaurus sp. is suggestive of correlation with the Upper Edmonton Formation, Alberta.
