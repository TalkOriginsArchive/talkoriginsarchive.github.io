Neocatastrophism

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/neocatastrophism.html

Neocatastrophism refers to the hypothesis that Earth's geological history has been shaped by sudden, large-scale catastrophic events rather than solely by gradual processes. This concept intersects with debates on deep time, uniformitarianism, and the scientific method within earth history. The bibliography includes sources discussing its application in stratigraphy and archaeology, as well as critical analyses of the theory's validity in explaining geological and historical records.
