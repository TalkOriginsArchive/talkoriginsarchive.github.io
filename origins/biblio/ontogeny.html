Ontogeny

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/ontogeny.html

Ontogeny refers to the development of an individual organism, a concept central to evolutionary biology through its relationship with phylogeny. This bibliography highlights Stephen Jay Gould’s influential work on heterochrony and the distinction between developmental stages and evolutionary history. It addresses the historical "recapitulation" theory and modern debates regarding how ontogenetic processes inform our understanding of deep time and the scientific method in origins research.
