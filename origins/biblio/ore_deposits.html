TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Ore deposits

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/ore_deposits.html

Ore deposits provide critical evidence for deep time and plate tectonics, illustrating how geological processes operate over vast scales. This bibliography includes scientific literature on strata-bound deposits and rift-related mineralization, alongside creationist claims that attempt to explain these features through recent, catastrophic events like Noah's Flood. The collection serves to contrast standard geological models with antievolutionary interpretations within the creation/evolution controversy.

1. Kanasewich, E. R., 1968, Precambrian Rift: Genesis of Strata-Bound Ore Deposits: Science: v. 161, no. 3845: p. 1002-1005.

DOI: 10.1126/science.161.3845.1002

Abstract

Study of deep seismic reflections has detected a Precambrian rift valley below flat-lying sediments in southern Alberta. The anomalous magnetic and gravity trends show that the rift is continuous across Alberta and British Columbia (through the Kimberley lead-zinc field) and possibly the Coeur d'Alene mining district of Idaho. There is evidence that these ore bodies were deposited in a Precambrian rift under conditions similar to those prevailing in the hot-brine areas of the modern Red Sea.
