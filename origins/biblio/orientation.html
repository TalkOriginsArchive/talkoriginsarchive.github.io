TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Orientation

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/orientation.html

Orientation refers to the navigational mechanisms organisms use to maintain direction during movement, a concept central to understanding animal behavior and migration. This bibliography curates scientific literature on fish orientation and migration, primarily drawing from the Fish Physiology series. While these sources provide detailed biological insights into sensory systems and environmental relations, they are not directly relevant to origins, evolution, or the creation/evolution controversy.
