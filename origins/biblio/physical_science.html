TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Physical science

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/physical_science.html

Physical science serves as a broad category for evidence and arguments concerning the natural world, often invoked in debates over deep time, origins, and the scientific method. This bibliography includes sources ranging from general scientific literature to specific critiques found in creationist works, such as Henry Morris’s The Physical Sciences. It aims to provide context for how physical science is interpreted within the creation/evolution controversy and science education discussions.

4. Orens, Irving P., 1948, Physical Science and the Social Sciences: Philosophy of Science: v. 15, no. 2: p. 90-95.

DOI: 10.1086/286976

Abstract

The very juxtaposition of the terms “physical science” and “social sciences” in the same sentence is indicative of the definitive trend now present in both physical science and in the thinking of the physical scientist. The two fields of human interest represented by physical science and the social sciences have drawn closer together, have coalesced at least in those areas of implication deducible from the fields themselves and this conjunction is fraught with consequences important to both fields.
