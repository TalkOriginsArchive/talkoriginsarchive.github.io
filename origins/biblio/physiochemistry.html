Physiochemistry

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/physiochemistry.html

Physiochemistry refers to the study of chemical processes in biological systems, often focusing on self-assembly and molecular interactions. This bibliography collects sources on physicochemical mechanisms relevant to the origins of life and the evolution of complex biological structures. It includes literature on material self-assembly, peptide organization, and interface dynamics, providing scientific context for discussions on abiogenesis and the physical basis of life.

3. Hideyuki Ogawa, Hideyuki Ogawa and Takumi Takamura, Takumi Takamura and Yuhei Shimoyama, Yuhei Shimoyama, 1999, Self-Assembly Process of Alkanethiol Monolayers: Japanese Journal of Applied Physics: v. 38, no. 10R: p. 6019.

DOI: 10.1143/jjap.38.6019

Abstract

To reveal formation of self-assembled monolayers (SAM), we analyzed the molecular orientation of SAMs of alkanethiol and the molecular organization in the Langmuir-Blodgett (LB) and vacuum deposited (VD) films by Fourier-transform infrared reflection absorption spectroscopy (FT-IR-RAS) and X-ray diffraction (XRD). XRD of the LB films suggests that alkanethiol orients normal to a gold substrate. Alkanethiol in the VD films tilts with a larger angle on the gold than on the glass substrates. The FT-IR-RAS spectra revealed that the molecular organization in the SAM on the gold (111) surface is dependent on the immersion time. Alkanethiol in the SAM aligns normal to the substrate, when the molecule undergoes close packing. A stable SAM embedded in a well-ordered monolayer is controlled by the intermolecular interaction of alkanethiol as well as the interaction between sulfur and gold.

8. Venugopal, Malini, 2019, Self Assembly Peptides- Biomimetic Material: Open Access Journal of Dental Sciences: v. 4, no. 2: p. 1-3.

DOI: 10.23880/oajds-16000227

Abstract

Self-assembling peptides are peptides which undergo spontaneous assembling into ordered nanostructures. As dentistry we are moving towards minimal invasive methods and hence there is a growing interest with regard to this new material. Self assembling peptides can prevent dental caries at an early stage thus preventing surgical process of cavity preparation and restorations.
