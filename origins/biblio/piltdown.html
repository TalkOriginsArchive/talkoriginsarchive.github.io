TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Piltdown

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/piltdown.html

Piltdown refers to the infamous 1912 forgery of human fossils that misled paleoanthropology for decades. This bibliography collects sources discussing the hoax’s exposure, its impact on the study of human evolution, and its significance in the history of science. It serves as a reference for understanding how scientific fraud was uncovered and how it influenced public and professional perceptions of deep time and human origins.

5. Smith, Fred H., 2018, Piltdown: The International Encyclopedia of Biological Anthropology: p. 1-3.

DOI: 10.1002/9781118584538.ieba0383

Abstract

The Piltdown forgery is likely the most well‐known hoax in the history of science. The scientific unveiling of the Piltdown skull in 1912 set in motion a series of events that impacted the study of human evolution for 40 years. Classified as “ Eoanthropus dawsoni ” and hailed as “The First Englishman,” Piltdown was supported by many scholars but questioned by many others. Accumulating scientific information ultimately led to the exposure of the forgery and the end of the Piltdown question in 1953. Though often cited as a criticism of the efficacy of human evolutionary studies, Piltdown actually serves as an excellent example of the self‐correcting nature and methodological approach of science.
