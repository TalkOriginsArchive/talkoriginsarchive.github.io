TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Polar

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/polar.html

Polar references scientific literature on paleoclimatology, paleobotany, and plate tectonics, particularly focusing on ancient polar regions and "true polar wander." These sources provide evidence for deep time, continental drift, and past climates, often addressing claims about Earth's history relevant to the creation/evolution controversy.

5. Jurdy, D. M. and Van der Voo, R., 1975, True Polar Wander since the Early Cretaceous: Science: v. 187, no. 4182: p. 1193-1196.

DOI: 10.1126/science.187.4182.1193

Abstract

The motions of the lithospheric plates have been reconstructed for three time intervals back to the Early Cretaceous. These displacements were analyzed to determine the best-fitting rigid rotation, which could then be ascribed to true polar wander. The true polar wander so obtained is no larger than a few degrees and is within the magnitude of the uncertainties involved.
