Polar wandering

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/polar_wandering.html

Polar wandering refers to the apparent movement of Earth's magnetic poles over geological time, a phenomenon explained by plate tectonics and continental drift rather than actual pole shifts. This concept provides critical paleomagnetic evidence for deep time and the dynamic nature of Earth's crust. The bibliography includes sources on the scientific method used to reconstruct past climates and continental positions, addressing historical claims often misinterpreted in creationist arguments.
