TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Prehistory

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/prehistory.html

Prehistory encompasses the study of Earth’s history before written records, including the origins of life and the development of early human cultures. This bibliography collects sources on the conceptual history of the term, geological timelines, and the intersection of archaeology with origins science. It addresses how the "idea of prehistory" has evolved in scientific and public discourse, often intersecting with debates on deep time and the creation/evolution controversy.

6. Barnes, Gina L., 1990, The ‘idea of prehistory’ in Japan: Antiquity: v. 64, no. 245: p. 929-940.

DOI: 10.1017/s0003598x00079047

Abstract

Thirty years after Glyn Daniel's perceptive publication on the ‘idea of prehistory’ (Daniel 1962), the topic is enjoying a return engagement in the archaeological literature. Not only have the sources of the words for ‘prehistory’ been traced in various languages (Chippindale 1988; Clermont &amp; Smith 1990). but a new nuance has been added to the word ‘idea’. In the new chapters added to the re-issue of Daniel’s book, Renfrew uses the phrase ‘idea of prehistory’ to mean ‘a picture of the past’ or a ‘reconstruction of the past’ (Daniel &amp; Renfrew 1988: 198, 203). In other words, Renfrew has subtly shifted the meaning of ‘idea’ from the concept of a time of human existence before the advent of written history, as I perceive Daniel originally used it, to an interpretation of what went on in that time period.
