Reptile/mammal transitions

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/reptile_mammal_transitions.html

Reptile/mammal transitions refers to the evolutionary series of synapsids, often historically termed “mammal-like reptiles,” that document the gradual acquisition of mammalian traits from earlier amniotes. This bibliography collects scientific literature on this deep-time transition, addressing key evidence in earth history and paleontology. It also includes citations relevant to the creation/evolution controversy, such as works by Gish, to contextualize public claims about these origins.

19. Sues, Hans‐Dieter, 2001, Synapsida (‘Mammal‐like Reptiles’): Encyclopedia of Life Sciences.

DOI: 10.1038/npg.els.0001547

Abstract

In the phylogenetic system, the Synapsida include Mammalia and all other amniotes more closely related to Mammalia than to Reptilia. Nonmammalian synapsids form an extensive series of taxa that are successively more closely related to mammals and are often collectively referred to as ‘mammal‐like reptiles’. This vernacular term alludes to the superficially ‘reptilian’ appearance of many primitive synapsids. It has become scientifically meaningless because Synapsida and Reptilia are now interpreted as two entirely distinct evolutionary lineages of Amniota.
