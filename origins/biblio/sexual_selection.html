Sexual selection

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/sexual_selection.html

Sexual selection is a key mechanism in evolutionary theory, explaining how traits evolve through mate choice and competition rather than survival alone. This bibliography collects sources on sexual selection, dimorphism, and social organization, particularly in primates, reflecting its importance in understanding human origins and biological diversity. The citations span historical reviews and modern research, illustrating the concept’s enduring relevance in evolutionary biology and the broader science education context.
