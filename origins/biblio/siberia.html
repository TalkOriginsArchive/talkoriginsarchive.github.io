Siberia

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/siberia.html

Siberia is a key geological region for understanding deep time, particularly through its extensive Precambrian and Cambrian stratigraphy. This bibliography compiles citations regarding the area’s microfossils, carbon isotope records, and petroleum geology, which provide critical evidence for early life and Earth’s history. These sources support scientific models of evolution and geological development against creationist narratives.

A high-resolution carbon isotope profile through the uppermost Neoproterozoic–Lower Cambrian part of the Sukharikha section at the northwestern margin of the Siberian platform shows prominent secular oscillations of δ 13 C with peak-to-peak range of 6–10 ‰. It consists of four excursions with four major maxima that can be correlated with Tommotian–Botomian peaks II, IV, V, and VII of the reference profile from the southeastern Siberian platform. Abstract

A number of ecological and geochemical transformations occurred during late Ediacaran and early Cambrian time, the effects of which are difficult to overestimate. Abstract
