Siberian platform

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/siberian_platform.html

Siberian platform is a major geological craton in Russia, central to research on Precambrian and Cambrian stratigraphy, microfossils, and deep-time earth history. This bibliography collects scientific literature detailing its geological evolution, paleoenvironments, and biostratigraphic records. These sources provide critical evidence for understanding the development of early life and the geological timeline, distinct from creationist narratives.

Abstract

The former stratigraphic chart of the Cambrian in the Turukhansk–Irkutsk–Olekma region of the Siberian Platform was compiled in 1986 and approved in 1988. Modifications necessitated by new data entail an indefinitely continuous series of revised facies zonation, recognition of new facies areas, regions and zone, as well as definition of a regional early Mayan stratigraphic hiatus.
