TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Social darwinism

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/social_darwinism.html

Social darwinism refers to the historical misapplication of evolutionary concepts to human societies, often used to justify social policies or economic theories. This bibliography collects sources examining these claims, ranging from late 19th-century texts to modern critiques in evolutionary economics and ethics. It serves to clarify the distinction between biological evolution and sociopolitical ideologies within the context of the creation/evolution controversy and science education.
