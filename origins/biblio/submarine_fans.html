TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Submarine fans

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/submarine_fans.html

Submarine fans are large underwater sedimentary deposits formed by turbidity currents, serving as key evidence for deep time and sedimentary processes in earth history. This bibliography collects geological and geophysical literature analyzing their formation, stratigraphy, and role in interpreting the geological record. These sources support scientific understanding of ocean basin evolution and provide context for discussions on origins and earth history.

15. Bush, Susan M., 1989, Submarine fans: Eos, Transactions American Geophysical Union: v. 70, no. 47: p. 1504-1504.

DOI: 10.1029/89eo00360

Abstract

Seisimic evidence from a study of 35‐m.y.‐old sediment contradicts the prevailing theory that bottom currents are the main cause of sediment distribution patterns and the shape of the continental rise. In the research area 200 miles offshore from New Jersey to Cape Hatteras, N.C., Stanley D. Locker of the University of South Florida, St. Petersburg, has found that submarine fans and deep‐sea sediment drifts may develop simultaneously as companion systems. He presented his work at the Geological Society of America meeting in St. Louis, Mo., November 6–9.
