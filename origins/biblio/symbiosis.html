Symbiosis

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/symbiosis.html

Symbiosis refers to the close, long-term biological interactions between different species, a concept central to understanding the evolution of complex life. This bibliography focuses on symbiogenesis, particularly Lynn Margulis’s theory that eukaryotic cells evolved through the merging of simpler organisms. It addresses how this mechanism explains key transitions in earth history and cell evolution, often contrasting with gradualist views in the creation/evolution controversy.
