Triclads

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/triclads.html

Triclads refers to freshwater planarians, flatworms historically studied for their regenerative abilities and reproductive biology. This bibliography compiles scientific literature on their ecology, phylogeny, and competitive interactions. While primarily focused on biological research, these sources provide context for understanding evolutionary relationships and species diversity within aquatic ecosystems.

1. Ullyott, P. and Beauchamp, R. S. A., 1931, Mechanisms for the Prevention of Self-Fertilization in some Species of Fresh-Water Triclads: Journal of Cell Science: v. S2-74, no. 295: p. 477-490.

DOI: 10.1242/jcs.s2-74.295.477

Abstract

Experiments by Gelei (1924) show that some very effective mechanism for the prevention of self-fertilization does exist in Dendrocoelum lacteum. When kept alone from the time of hatching, this species produced cocoons containing eggs which had not been fertilized. Two such animals which had both produced unfertile eggs while isolated, after pairing laid cocoons containing fertile eggs.
