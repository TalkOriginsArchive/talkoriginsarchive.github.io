TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Vendian

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/vendian.html

Vendian refers to a historical geological term for the latest stage of the Proterozoic Eon, preceding the Cambrian explosion. This bibliography collects sources on Vendian stratigraphy, paleontology, and correlation with the Cambrian, primarily from Russian and international geological literature. These citations support the scientific understanding of deep time and the Precambrian-Cambrian transition, countering creationist claims of a young Earth or sudden appearance of complex life.

13. Eganov, E. A., 1995, GEOLOGICAL POSITION OF PHOSPHORITE DEPOSITS IN VENDIAN-CAMBRIAN STRATA: Russian Geology and Geophysics: v. 36, no. 9: p. 1-13.

DOI: 10.2113/rgg.1995.36.9.1

Abstract

The article analyzes the geological position of commercially significant phosphorite deposits in thick ancient carbonate strata (from Riphean to Cambrian age) with respect to reference phosphorite-bearing formations in basins of Siberia, Kazakhstan, Mongolia, and China. The position of productive horizons in cross sections of carbonate strata is located more precisely, and the structure of preore, ore, and postore sedimentation cycles is described, as well as characteristic indicators of the probable presence and location of phosphorites in ancient carbonate strata of the Asiatic phosphate-accumulation province, particularly in Siberia and the Far East.
