Vestigial organs

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/vestigial_organs.html

Vestigial organs are anatomical structures that have lost most or all of their original function through evolution, serving as key evidence for common descent and deep time. This bibliography collects scientific literature and historical sources discussing rudimentary structures in humans and other animals, such as whale pelvises and human tails. These citations address the creation/evolution controversy by illustrating how evolutionary theory explains non-functional traits, while also covering related medical and paleontological research.
