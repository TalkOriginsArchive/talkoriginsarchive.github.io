TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Who's who

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/who's_who.html

Who&#x27;s who refers to biographical reference works that list notable individuals across various professions and fields. While these sources provide factual data on people’s careers and achievements, they do not inherently address scientific evidence for evolution, earth history, or the creation/evolution controversy. This bibliography collects such citations primarily for their utility in verifying the backgrounds of individuals involved in public debates or historical contexts, rather than as primary scientific literature.

4. Wenzel, Sarah, 2019, World Who’s Who: The Charleston Advisor: v. 20, no. 4: p. 56-58.

DOI: 10.5260/chara.20.4.56

Abstract

This compilation of Who’s Who pulls together biographies of approximately 70,000 men and women in a variety of fields. Entries include, when available, such information as nationality, profession, date and place of birth or death, education, family details, career, awards, publications, artistic achievements, and contact information. The interface imitates a print book in many ways; however, that does not impede searching for and discovering the information included.
