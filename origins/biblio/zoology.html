TalkOrigins Bibliography Topic

Zoology

Bibliography index · /origins/biblio/zoology.html

Zoology is the study of animal life, encompassing anatomy, behavior, classification, and ecology. This bibliography includes foundational texts and historical works that explore animal distribution, abundance, and evolutionary adaptations, providing insights into the biological diversity of Earth's fauna. It also features popular science accounts that have contributed to public understanding of zoology, making it relevant to discussions on evolution, deep time, and the history of scientific thought.

Abstract

‘The appreciation and study of the Natural Sciences appear to have been of tardy growth in the North of Scotland’ (Traill 1906). The origins of the study of zoology in the Universities of Scotland are reminiscent of evolutionary events. A shadowy beginning, periods of apparent stagnation, sudden transition into more active states, and latterly, a reversion and regrouping under modern financial pressures. Not a smooth progression, but rather a lurching progress and cladistic development!
