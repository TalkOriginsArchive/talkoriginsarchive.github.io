Copyright & Fair Use
An excellent resource on the subject of copyright in general and the concept of "fair use."
  • All documents in this site are copyrighted by their individual authors. The collection of these documents is copyrighted by The Talk.Origins Archive.
  • This site has no objection to anyone making a link to any document in this site. Anyone may do so without asking permission.
  • Permission to reproduce documents in this Archive is granted for noncommercial personal or educational use provided that the text of documents are not altered or edited and a statement mentioning the copyright of the author(s) is included. If a copy is sold at more than cost or if the text of a document is edited in any way then one must first obtain permission of the author(s) of that document.
    • If a document has a copyright statement contrary to the above than that document's copyright statement supersedes this document for any statement that contradicts this document.
    • If a document indicates that it is a reprint of something previously published elsewhere then the copyright of the original source must be respected and its policies supersede this document.
  • Any fair use copying from this Archive must be appropriately attributed. Copy and pasting without attribution is simply unethical. Permission is denied for any copying from this site without attribution to either the author(s) or this site.
  • Any web site quoting part of a document from this site must either link back to the original or provide the URL of the original. There is simply no excuse for not doing so since linking is easy to do, takes very little time, and is proper netiquette. Permission to quote any part of this Archive is denied to any web site unwilling to make a link to or provide the URL of what it is quoting.
  • The Archive as no objection to the posting of translations of Archive documents and allows it on the same conditions for the reproduction of articles listed above provided that a good faith attempt is made at an accurate translation, a link is provided to the original English language document, an attempt is made to contact the author(s), and that the wishes of the author(s) are respected. If a translation is put online please tell us so this site has the option of linking to the translation.
